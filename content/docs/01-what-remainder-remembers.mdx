In cryptography we base our computations and protocols on integers. We do this because fundamental pillars of cryptography such as Number Theory and Boolean Logic were built on top of discrete and countable numbers

Working with discrete numbers is not that different then working with rationals or real numbers (3/5 or Pi) - we add, multiple and subtract reals and integers just the same. Difference comes when we try to divide integers - because if you divide 12 / 5 and want to get an integer... well how do you actually do that?

Actually a CPU on your device already does it. It simply calculates integer value out of division and checks what is left - getting a remainder. So 12 / 5 is simply:

```
12 / 5 = 2 (and remainder of 2)
```

That's how integer division works in CPUs

So you have 2 operators - modulo and full integer division
